**Format:** `[TIME] SHOT (what's on screen)` / *VOICEOVER (what's said).*

### [0:00–0:18] — Landing page

**SHOT:** The Hxrizxn landing page. Slow zoom on the headline and the "solve for x" tagline.

> *"Every big decision is an equation with missing variables. Most AI tools hand you a confident answer and hide the uncertainty. Hxrizxn does the opposite — it's a multi-agent reasoning system that works a hard decision across futures, shows its evidence, and admits what it can't know. Built on Microsoft Foundry."*

---

### [0:18–0:40] — Intake page

**SHOT:** Click into the intake. Type a real enterprise decision:
> *"Should we accept a $900k enterprise contract with heavy custom features that delays our roadmap six months and risks engineering burnout?"*
Fill goals (revenue, proof point) and fears (burnout, roadmap delay, customer concentration). Click Analyze.

> *"You describe the decision in plain language — your goals, your fears, your constraints. I'll use a real enterprise dilemma: a big contract that could bring in nine hundred thousand dollars, but delays the roadmap and risks burning out the team. One click, and eleven specialized agents go to work."*

---

### [0:40–1:15] — Loading page · the "Behind the scenes" trace

**SHOT:** The loading screen with the live agent trace. Let it populate; move the cursor down the list as each agent completes with its real latency.

> *"This is the reasoning happening in real time. Not one prompt pretending to think — eleven distinct agents, each with a job. Decision Framing turns the messy story into a solvable problem. Evidence Grounding pulls cited knowledge from Foundry IQ. The Assumption Miner surfaces what you're taking for granted. The Scenario Lattice branches into optimistic, base, and stress futures. Then ripple effects, regret and reversibility, black-swan risks, a future-self check, an experiment designer, and a safety gate — each step real, timed, and inspectable."*

---

### [1:15–1:40] — Scenarios page (Comparison)

**SHOT:** The Scenarios screen — the three futures with scorecards, the radar/optionality visuals.

> *"It doesn't give you one answer. It computes three futures — best case, base case, and stress case — each scored on financial trajectory, reversibility, and how aligned it is with your goals. You see the whole decision space, not a single confident guess."*

---

### [1:40–2:20] — Ripple page · Evidence & Honesty panel (THE KEY MOMENT)

**SHOT:** The Ripple Effects screen. Show the risk heatmap briefly, then scroll to the **Evidence & Honesty** panel. Point cursor at a green **Grounded** badge with its source, then an amber **Unverified** badge, then the black-swan risk still amber.

> *"And here's what makes it trustworthy. Every assumption and every risk is checked against the Foundry IQ knowledge base. When there's evidence, the agent grounds the claim and cites the source — like 'build-versus-buy total cost of ownership.' When there isn't, it marks the claim Unverified and abstains — it refuses to assert what it can't back up. Notice the black-swan risk stays unverified on purpose: speculation is labeled as speculation. This is anti-hallucination built into the reasoning itself."*

---

### [2:20–2:35] — Experiment page

**SHOT:** The Experiment Plan screen.

> *"Instead of forcing an irreversible choice, it designs the cheapest reversible test — a time-boxed pilot with a pre-committed kill metric. Don't bet the company; rent the future for thirty days first."*

### [2:35–2:55] — Memo page

**SHOT:** The Decision Memo screen — recommendation, probability bands, citations, disclaimers.

> *"It lands on a recommendation — but an honest one. Probability bands, not false precision. Cited evidence. Clear disclaimers. And the same engine that reasoned through this contract also handles personal and even emotional decisions — proof the reasoning generalizes."*
