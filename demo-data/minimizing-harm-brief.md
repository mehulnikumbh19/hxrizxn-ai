# Minimizing Harm When Ending a Relationship

When a choice will hurt a partner, harm is reduced by honesty, taking responsibility, avoiding prolonged uncertainty, and setting clear boundaries.

Guidance:
- Delaying a decision tends to deepen resentment and distress for everyone involved.
- Ambiguity is often more painful than a clear, kind ending.
- Take responsibility for your part rather than blaming circumstances.
- Give the other person clarity so they can begin their own recovery.
